the director word

The director of the center
Osama Hashem Al-Hadidi

Praise be to Allah, and peace and blessings be upon our Prophet Muhammad, his family, companions, and those who follow them. The Al-Azhar International Center for Electronic Fatwa, one of the departments of Al-Azhar and its sectors, is concerned with issuing fatwas in society, correcting misconceptions, and confronting extremist ideas. It was established under the noble guidance of His Eminence Sheikh Ahmed Al-Tayeb, the Grand Imam of Al-Azhar, in the year 2016. The objectives of the center can be summarized as follows:
• Confronting the chaos of fatwas and deviant and aberrant ideas by filling the scientific and jurisprudential void with the moderate approach and moderate thinking.
• Reaching all segments of society in various age groups, especially youth, through the latest contemporary technological means.
• Increasing the number of beneficiaries of fatwas and facilitating access for Muslims through the pluralism of Al-Azhar's jurisprudence.
• Responding to doubts, correcting concepts, and meeting the needs of non-Arabic speakers.
• Disseminating moderate thinking through moderate fatwas to achieve intellectual security and social stability by promoting moderate fatwas according to the approach of Al-Azhar.
• Confronting extremism and terrorism by dismantling destructive ideas that call for violence and terrorism, through high-precision monitoring units whose members are highly competent in cooperation with other departments of the center.
• Reinforcing virtuous values and building enlightened Muslim mindsets.
• Cementing national principles and working on the cohesion of society and family bonds.
• Training and qualifying specialized scientific and media cadres.
• Addressing social phenomena and emerging issues scientifically and practically by reaching out to all segments of society and scientific institutions such as universities.
• Confronting the most common problems brought to the center, such as the problem of divorce, and establishing units and programs to help solve these problems, such as the reconciliation unit, family and community awareness programs, and a unit for confronting atheism and secular thought.
The center has made many efforts to achieve these goals, including:
• Issuing more than 1.2 million fatwas through phone fatwas, applications, email, social media conversations, websites, newspapers, and channels to deliver the correct fatwa to the inquirer easily.
• Responding to all misconceptions and extremist fatwas received by the center, refuting them, and correcting the concepts of their proponents.
• Introducing the true religion through awareness publications on the center's various electronic platforms.
• Holding public meetings and awareness seminars aimed at refuting misconceptions, extremist concepts, and addressing
the questions of the public, with more than 46,200 public meetings held until January 30, 2022.
• Conducting awareness campaigns using modern means to reach the largest number of followers, and promoting these campaigns through various channels, newspapers, and websites.
• Mediating reconciliation through the reconciliation unit to address the phenomenon of divorce, with the unit intervening in more than 68,000 family disputes and achieving reconciliation in more than 67,000 cases by January 30, 2022.
• Continuously monitoring extremist fatwas on websites and social media platforms, and holding workshops to inform center members of what is found in this regard.
These efforts aim to achieve the vision set by the center, which is to eliminate the chaos of fatwas and eradicate extremist thinking, making it truly a tool for Al-Azhar to confront the chaos of fatwas and eradicate extremist thinking.
